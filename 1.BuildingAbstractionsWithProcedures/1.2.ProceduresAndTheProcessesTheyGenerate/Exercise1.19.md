# Exercise 1.19

We have
$$
\begin{aligned}
  &a_{1}=(p+q)a_{0}+qb_{0} \\
  &b_{1}=qa_{0}+pb_{0}
\end{aligned}
$$
And
$$
\begin{aligned}
  &a_{2}=(p+q)a_{1}+qb_{1}=(p^2+2pq+2q^2)a_{0}+(2pq+q^2)b_{0} \\
  &b_{2}=qa_{1}+pb_{1}=(q^2+2pq)a_{0}+(p^2+q^2)b_{0}
\end{aligned}
$$
So we can infer that
$$
\begin{aligned}
  &p'\leftarrow p^2+q^2 \\
  &q'\leftarrow 2pq+q^2
\end{aligned}
$$
